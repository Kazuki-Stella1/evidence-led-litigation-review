## Evidence–claim matrix

Use one row for each proposition that matters to liability, causation, or damages.

| Proposition | Direct exhibit and page | What it directly shows | Inference, if any | Expected response | Status |

Do not put a legal conclusion in the “what it directly shows” column. If an exhibit proves only timing, notice, or a speaker’s statement, say so.

## Issue chain

```text
chronology
  -> knowable circumstances
  -> governing standard
  -> act or omission
  -> breach or wrongfulness
  -> factual and legal causation
  -> damage or remedy
```

Mark every link as supported, inferential, disputed, or missing.

## Filing-day gate

- No unresolved exhibit, date, amount, or party-name placeholder.
- Relief and conclusion state the same amount, interest start, and rate.
- Every exhibit reference exists in the evidence explanation.
- Distinct medical events and damage periods remain distinct.
- Arithmetic and present-value factors have been recalculated.
- The strongest jurisdiction, duty, lawful-advocacy, causation, and amount defenses are addressed.
- The rendered filing has been inspected page by page.
